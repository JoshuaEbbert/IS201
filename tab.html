Age and Olympic Athletes

Created by Joshua Ebbert

Breakdown

We see a mean approximately at 25 years of age with outlier countries showing the average age to be as high as 34 years old for their athletes. The lowest average, displayed by Botswana, dips beneath 20 years of age to a shocking 18 years of age.

When testing something as age-dependent such as physical prowess and technique, it makes sense that age would be so closely clustered around the mid-twenties. The prime of athleticism tends to find itself in this span, although it can vary by sport. Age-dependent factors include the following:

  1. Risk of injury
    • Loss of flexibility
    • Susceptibility of joints to tissue damage
    • Complication of other health challenges
  2. Time needed for recovery
    • Increased time between practice sessions
    • Increased need for sleep
  3. Physical strength
    • Maximum athletic strength
    • Strength endurance
    • Explosive strength
  4. Coordination
    • Hand-eye skills
    • Gross motor skills
    • Fine motor skills
